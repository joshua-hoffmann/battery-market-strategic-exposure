# V2.10 Cross-Mineral Traceability Summary Note

## Purpose

This note summarizes the traceability state of the V2.4 downstream concentration comparison after the V2.5 to V2.9 source-enrichment work.

It does not add new mineral concentration values and does not revise the V2.4 study. It classifies the current evidence state so future work can target the weakest source gaps.

## Inputs

- data/processed/v24_downstream_concentration_comparison.csv
- data/curated/v25_source_gap_enrichment_plan.csv
- data/curated/v28_lithium_iea_2025_traceability_register.csv
- data/curated/v29_manganese_definition_comparability_register.csv

## Output

- data/curated/v210_cross_mineral_traceability_summary.csv

## Summary classification

Lithium is the strongest downstream concentration case after V2.8 because the existing V2.4 processing/refining top-three value is source-traceable to IEA Lithium 2025.

Manganese remains analytically useful but proxy-limited after V2.9 because the downstream value concerns battery-grade or high-purity manganese sulphate rather than a fully comparable general manganese processing/refining denominator.

Natural graphite, cobalt, and nickel remain public-snapshot comparison cases. They can remain in V2.4, but their claims should not be strengthened until source traceability or product-definition detail is added.

## Accepted now

- V2.4 can be described as a bounded descriptive downstream concentration comparison.
- Lithium can be identified as source-traceable for the existing 2024 processing/refining value.
- Manganese can be retained with explicit proxy limitation.
- Natural graphite, cobalt, and nickel can remain public-snapshot cases with caveats.

## Not accepted now

- no investment signal;
- no policy recommendation;
- no geopolitical ranking;
- no bottleneck proof;
- no disruption probability;
- no vulnerability ranking;
- no complete supply-chain risk model;
- no claim that all minerals have equivalent source strength.

## Next work option

The best next source-enrichment target is natural graphite because it has a moderate downstream gap and likely product-definition issues similar to manganese, but less severe than the manganese proxy limitation.

A lower-priority alternative is cobalt or nickel precision traceability, but their small observed gaps make them less valuable unless the project specifically needs tighter small-gap interpretation.
