# V2.13 Natural Graphite Product-Definition Traceability Note

## Purpose

This note records the natural graphite product-definition traceability check following V2.10, V2.11, and V2.12.

It does not add a new mineral value, does not replace the V2.4 natural graphite value, and does not create a public-facing claim.

## Decision

Natural graphite should remain in the V2.4 comparison as a public-snapshot case with explicit caveats.

The main traceability improvement from V2.13 is product-definition clarity: natural graphite mining, natural flake graphite, synthetic graphite, spherical graphite, coated spherical graphite, battery-grade graphite, and anode material should not be treated as interchangeable denominators.

IEA context supports graphite as a relevant critical mineral and indicates that the IEA data environment is the right next verification target. Historical IEA material is useful because it links battery graphite processing to spherical graphite for battery grade. USGS is useful for natural graphite production and mining context, but it does not verify the processing/refining top-three share.

## Current status

- V2.4 natural graphite processing/refining value remains: 99.
- V2.10 traceability class remains: public_snapshot_needs_source_traceability_detail.
- V2.13 does not accept a new numeric value.
- V2.13 reduces the product-definition ambiguity but does not close the numeric traceability gap.

## Accepted now

- natural graphite is a valid next midstream source-enrichment target;
- product-definition alignment is required before strengthening the natural graphite claim;
- USGS can support natural graphite mining/production context;
- IEA is the stronger candidate for processing/refining or battery-grade spherical graphite denominator verification.

## Not accepted now

- no replacement of the V2.4 value 99;
- no claim that natural graphite mining equals spherical graphite processing;
- no claim that spherical graphite equals all graphite anode material;
- no claim that synthetic and natural graphite are interchangeable;
- no investment signal;
- no policy recommendation;
- no geopolitical ranking;
- no bottleneck proof;
- no disruption probability;
- no vulnerability ranking;
- no complete downstream model;
- no validated risk model.

## Next work option

The next source-enrichment step would be a manual IEA 2025 Data Explorer or report-table verification for natural graphite processing/refining. Without that table/export verification, the existing 99 value should remain public-snapshot only.
