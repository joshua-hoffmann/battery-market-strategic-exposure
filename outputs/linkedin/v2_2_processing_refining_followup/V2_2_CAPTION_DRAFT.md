In the previous post, I looked at mine-production concentration.

This follow-up asks whether concentration remains — or becomes stronger — after extraction.

Battery bottlenecks don’t stop at mining.

In this V2.2 case study, I compared 2024 mine-production concentration with processing/refining concentration across five battery minerals:

Lithium
Cobalt
Nickel
Natural graphite
Manganese

The simple question:

Where does concentration remain after extraction?

Under the allowed 2024 top-three-share comparison, all five minerals show higher processing/refining concentration than mine-production concentration.

But the gaps are not equal.

Largest downstream gaps:
Lithium: +21.83 pp
Manganese: +21.00 pp
Natural graphite: +9.38 pp

Smaller gaps:
Nickel: +1.51 pp
Cobalt: +0.48 pp

The takeaway is not that this is a risk score or investment signal.

It is a concentration snapshot.

The point is that a mining-only view can miss an important downstream bottleneck: who can process or refine mined material into usable battery inputs.
